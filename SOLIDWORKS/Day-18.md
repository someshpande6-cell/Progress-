Step-by-Step SolidWorks Modeling Explanation
Step 1: Base Plate (Boss-Extrude)
Start a sketch on the Top Plane.
Draw the base profile (rectangular with a rounded front end).
Include a circular extension at the front for the mounting hole.
Apply Boss-Extrude to create the base thickness.
Step 2: Front Hole (Cut-Extrude)
On the circular front portion, sketch a circle at the center.
Use Cut-Extrude → Through All to create the mounting hole.
Step 3: Inclined Support (Boss-Extrude)
On the top surface of the base, sketch a triangular profile.
This triangle connects the front circular portion to the rear vertical support.
Apply Boss-Extrude to create the inclined rib-like support.
Step 4: Rear Vertical Plate (Boss-Extrude)
Select the back edge of the base and create a sketch on the Right Plane or side face.
Draw a vertical plate with a rounded (arc) top profile.
Extrude using Boss-Extrude to form the rear support.
Step 5: Hole in Rear Plate (Cut-Extrude)
On the rear vertical plate, sketch a circle at the upper center.
Apply Cut-Extrude → Through All to create the hole.
Step 6: Middle Cylindrical Boss (Boss-Extrude)
On the inclined face or vertical plate junction, sketch a circle.
Apply Boss-Extrude to create a cylindrical boss protruding outward.
Step 7: Hole in Cylindrical Boss (Cut-Extrude)
Sketch a concentric circle on the cylindrical boss.
Use Cut-Extrude → Through All to create the hole.
Step 8: Rib Connection (Boss-Extrude)
Create a sketch connecting the front circular base to the middle cylindrical boss.
Draw a narrow triangular or rectangular rib.
Use Boss-Extrude to form the supporting rib.
Step 9: Side Wall Reinforcement (Boss-Extrude)
Add an additional inclined or vertical wall between base and rear plate.
Sketch on the side and extrude to strengthen the structure.
Step 10: Fillets and Finishing
Apply Fillet to edges where needed:
Base edges
Junctions of ribs and supports
This improves strength and removes sharp edges.
Final Summary

The model is a reinforced bracket structure created using multiple Boss-Extrude features for the base, supports, ribs, and cylindrical boss. Cut-Extrude operations are used to generate mounting holes. The design emphasizes structural strength using inclined ribs and vertical supports, commonly used in mechanical brackets.
