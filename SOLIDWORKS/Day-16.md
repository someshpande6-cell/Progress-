Step-by-Step SolidWorks Modeling Explanation

Step 1: Base Sketch (Top Plane)
Start a sketch on the Top Plane.
Draw a central circle of Ø40 mm.
Around it, draw another circle of Ø62 mm to form the outer ring.
This will act as the main hub of the model.

Step 2: Bolt Circle and Holes
Create a bolt circle and sketch 6 equally spaced holes around the center.
Each hole has a diameter of Ø8 mm.
Use Circular Sketch Pattern to maintain equal spacing.

Step 3: Outer Small Circular Features
Around the outer ring, draw 6 small circular bosses:
Each outer circle is Ø16 mm.
Position them symmetrically using circular pattern or equal spacing constraints.

Step 4: Lower Base Circles
At the bottom portion, sketch 3 larger circles:
Outer diameter: Ø30 mm
Inner hole: Ø20 mm
Maintain equal horizontal spacing:
Distance between centers: 55 mm

Step 5: Side Connections (Arcs & Curves)
Connect the top circular hub to the lower circles using tangent arcs.
Use smooth curves with radius:
Approx. Ø36 mm arc on sides
Ensure all curves are tangent for smooth geometry.

Step 6: Vertical Positioning
Set vertical distance from top hub to lower circles:
65 mm
Use construction lines to align centers properly.

Step 7: Fully Define Sketch
Apply all dimensions:
Ø62, Ø40, Ø16, Ø30, Ø20, Ø8
Distances: 55 mm, 65 mm
Use Smart Dimension and geometric relations to fully constrain the sketch.

Step 8: Boss-Extrude
Exit sketch and apply Extruded Boss/Base.
Extrude to required thickness (e.g., 10–15 mm).

Step 9: Cut-Extrude (Holes)
Select all inner circles:
Central hole
Bolt holes
Bottom holes
Apply Cut-Extrude → Through All.
Step 10: Finishing
Add Fillets if required for smooth edges.
Ensure symmetry and clean transitions.
Final Summary

The model is created using a central hub with a bolt circle pattern, combined with symmetrical outer bosses and lower support circles. The design relies heavily on circular patterns, tangent arcs, and proper dimensioning to achieve a balanced and fully defined geometry.
